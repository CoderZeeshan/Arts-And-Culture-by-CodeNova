Karnataka Historical Timeline

Explore the rich dynasties, temples, and milestones that shaped Karnataka over centuries.

Historical Timeline

Mauryan Influence (3rd Century BCE)

The Mauryan Empire extended its influence into parts of Karnataka, introducing early administrative systems and Buddhist culture.

Satavahanas & Early Dynasties (2nd Century BCE - 3rd Century CE)

The Satavahanas ruled over northern Karnataka, promoting trade, art, and early temple architecture.

Kadamba Dynasty (4th - 6th Century CE)

Kadambas established the first native kingdom in Karnataka, with their capital at Banavasi, fostering early Kannada culture.

Chalukya Dynasty (6th - 12th Century CE)

Known for their architectural marvels like Badami caves, Pattadakal monuments, and for flourishing trade and literature.

Rashtrakutas (8th - 10th Century CE)

Famous for the Kailasanatha temple at Ellora and for patronizing art, literature, and Kannada poetry.

Western Chalukyas (10th - 12th Century CE)

Developed distinctive temple architecture in places like Lakkundi and Gadag, and promoted Kannada literature.

Hoysala Empire (11th - 14th Century CE)

Known for exquisite temples like Belur and Halebidu, marking the peak of Karnataka's temple architecture and art.

Vijayanagara Empire (14th - 17th Century CE)

One of the greatest South Indian kingdoms, leaving behind the UNESCO World Heritage site of Hampi and flourishing trade and culture.

Mysore Kingdom (17th - 20th Century CE)

Rulers like Tipu Sultan and Wodeyars promoted arts, culture, and administration. Mysore Palace became a symbol of grandeur.

Modern Karnataka (20th Century - Present)

Formation of the state of Karnataka in 1956, continued growth of technology, education, and cultural preservation.
